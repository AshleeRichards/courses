Journey Through New Zealand

New Zealand is a sovereign island country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean. The country geographically comprises two main landmasses—the North Island (Te Ika-a-Māui), and the South Island (Te Waipounamu)—and around 600 smaller islands.

I was fortunate enough to call Auckland home for over a year, and look forward to sharing the country's hidden gems with you all.

From Tents to Resorts... and everything in between

New Zealand is full of wonderful places to stay. You have the ability to sleep in the outdoors in a tent, a star-gazing glass-enclosed Pure Pod, or relax like a king at a five star resort. Here are my top three recomendations:

  • Tawharanui Peninsula Campground
  • Pure Pod New Zealand
  • Queenstown Hotel

Pack Accordingly

New Zealand is home to every type of climate and environment one can imagine. You need to prepare for a range of temperatures and conditions. Here are a few tips:

  1. Bring layers of clothing
  2. Pack sunscreen (seriously!)
  3. Always carry a rainjacket
  4. Wear sturdy footwear
